## Display Artist Name Romanisation Changes on Bandcamp

So far, we've been romanizing artist names (mainly Japanese ones) for a better reach of our past audience, mainly being English-speaking. We believe using appropriately romanized names (and confirmed with their respective artists) means a better understanding/reading of artist names which may be impossible to read for non Japanese-speaking readers. However, this might come at the expense of artist names' original intentions, whether due to stylization or/and metadata purposes. Therefore, **from now on, we will generally stick to originally input artist names on Bandcamp, similarly to BOOTH**. This will not apply for track names, however - just Display Artist names. **Detailed track description/credits on the Website and Bandcamp, however, will always display a latin reading of the name,** whether it is intentionally left in japanese or not, for referencing/detailed information. What's mainly concerned is the "display" artist field, visible on Websites, stores, and track metadata. As much as I would love to display both similarly to how furigana is used, like we do with video and images - it's not exactly possible on a plaintext system, and we have to compromise in some way. I believe that the reading of non-latin characters is very important, especially in our case so far since they have been taken directly to the source and confirmed, while keeping artist names displayed as they were intended by their respective artists - same as track name transliterations and translations. **This change will not be applied retroactively, and we currently have no plans to change albums already published with their current metadata**. That goes against my general principles of keeping our releases stuck to their time, the same way we do not change metadata simply because of an artist/alias name change. Being an editor/enthusiast of VGMdb, and not being fluent in japanese, I have so far applied my own reasoning on song metadata to a very strict degree - and have applied it to my own circle. However, with the change of scope and audience KodamaSounds is going through, I believe sticking to the most fundamental standards of the Doujin/Japanese scene and requests from artists, it is for the best to proceed as such from now on. If you liked our previous way of handling our metadata, I invite you to retag our future releases on your own so it fits your needs.
